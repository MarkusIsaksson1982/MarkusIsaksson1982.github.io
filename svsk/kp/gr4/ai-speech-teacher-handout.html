Top 5 AI Recommendations

For Students

  1. Use AI for brainstorming & outlines — refine with peers.
  2. Record short practice runs; let AI flag unclear uttal.
  3. Get low-stakes feedback on pacing & structure before peer review.
  4. Ask AI to spot bias in sources.
  5. Keep an “AI journal” logging how you used tools.

For Teachers

  1. Use batch AI analysis to spot class-wide trends.
  2. Adjust lesson pacing with AI insights (structure vs. uttal).
  3. Apply one rubric spine across Svenska & SvA with weighted emphasis.
  4. Require AI-use logs for ethical transparency.
  5. Adapt model for other subjects (Samhällskunskap, Engelska, Historia).

Cross-Subject Scaling

Denna enhet är en low-barrier pilot för AI-literacy: Använd retoriska processen över språkämnen, forskning i samhällskunskap, eller "in character"-tal i historia.

Teacher's 5-Minute Kick-off Guide

  1. (1 min) Frame the 'Why': "Over the next two weeks, we'll be working on informative speeches. Our goal, according to Gy25, is for you to become more confident, adaptable speakers and more critical thinkers. To help us, we'll be using a new 'digital training partner'."
  2. (2.5 min) Show, Don't Tell: "Let's watch a short video that shows exactly how this process will work." Action: Play the demo video.
  3. (1 min) Introduce the Tools & Roles: "As you saw, you'll get feedback from three sources." Action: Point to student mini-poster. "You'll have an AI for technical feedback, your classmates for audience feedback, and most importantly, your own judgment to make the final call."
  4. (0.5 min) Set the Core Expectation: "Your goal is not to simply accept all feedback. Your goal is to *evaluate* the feedback. You are the director of your own speech. That final decision is the most important skill you'll practice."
