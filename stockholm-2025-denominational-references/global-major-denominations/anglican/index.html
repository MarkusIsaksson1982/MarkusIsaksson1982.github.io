Overview

The Anglican Communion is a worldwide fellowship of churches in the Anglican tradition, with approximately 85 million members globally. It maintains episcopal governance, historic liturgy, and the via media approach‚Äîholding a balance between Catholic and Protestant theological emphases. The Archbishop of Canterbury serves as the spiritual leader and symbol of unity, without exercising papal-style authority.

Comparison Axes (from manifest)

  • Ecclesiology: Episcopal structure with autonomous provinces; Archbishop of Canterbury as primus inter pares
  • Soteriology: Emphasis on justification by faith, personal and corporate holiness, sacramental grace mediated through episcopally ordained ministry
  • Sacramental Theology: Two primary sacraments (Baptism, Eucharist), additional sacramental rites recognized; Eucharist seen as real spiritual presence
  • Social Ethics: Justice, reconciliation, creation care, human rights, global development initiatives via Anglican Communion Office and regional councils
  • Ecumenical Theology: Porvoo Communion with Lutheran churches, dialogues with Roman Catholic, Orthodox, and Protestant communities; commitment to visible unity and mission
  • Global Context: Over 40 autonomous provinces; strong presence in UK, Africa, Asia, Oceania, and the Americas; diverse theological and cultural expressions within communion

Notes

Key documents include the Book of Common Prayer, Lambeth Conference Resolutions, and Anglican formularies. The Anglican Communion emphasizes contextual theology and local leadership within a global fellowship. For Stockholm 2025, Anglican participation demonstrates ecumenical engagement and collaboration with Lutheran, Catholic, and Orthodox traditions.
