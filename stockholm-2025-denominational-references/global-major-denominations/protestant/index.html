Overview

The global Protestant family encompasses multiple denominations, including Lutheran, Reformed, Methodist, Baptist, and other evangelical and mainline traditions. Membership is approximately 800 million worldwide. Governance structures vary from episcopal (e.g., Methodist) to presbyterian and congregational models. Protestant theology emphasizes scripture as primary authority, justification by faith alone, and the priesthood of all believers.

Comparison Axes (from manifest)

  • Ecclesiology: Diverse governance models: episcopal, presbyterian, and congregational structures; autonomous local congregations within broader denominational frameworks
  • Soteriology: Emphasis on salvation by grace through faith, personal conversion, discipleship, and social holiness
  • Sacramental Theology: Two main sacraments/ordinances: Baptism and Eucharist (Lord‚Äôs Supper); symbolic and spiritual presence interpretations; some variations by denomination
  • Social Ethics: Active engagement in social justice, humanitarian aid, global development, education, and advocacy for human rights and creation care
  • Ecumenical Theology: Engagement through World Communion of Reformed Churches, World Methodist Council, and other global ecumenical forums; emphasis on mission cooperation and theological dialogue
  • Global Context: Strongest presence in Europe, North and South America, Africa, and parts of Asia; highly diverse in worship style, theology, and social engagement

Notes

Key documents include confessional standards (e.g., Augsburg Confession, Westminster Confession), catechisms, and denominational statements of faith. Protestant traditions participating in Stockholm 2025 highlight ecumenical cooperation, shared worship, and social witness, connecting with Lutheran, Anglican, Catholic, Orthodox, and Pentecostal partners.
