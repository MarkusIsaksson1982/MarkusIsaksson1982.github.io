Overview

Pentecostalism is a worldwide movement emphasizing the baptism in the Holy Spirit, spiritual gifts, lively worship, and personal conversion. Estimated global membership exceeds 280 million, spanning all continents. Governance ranges from congregational autonomy to cooperative regional structures. Pentecostal theology prioritizes experiential faith, evangelism, and transformative social action.

Comparison Axes (from manifest)

  • Global Context: Strongest in Africa, Latin America, North America, and parts of Asia; high youth engagement, contemporary worship styles, and church planting movements
