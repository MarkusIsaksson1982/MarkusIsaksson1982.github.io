Overview

The Orthodox Churches in Sweden include parishes from various patriarchates (e.g., Constantinople, Antioch, Moscow, Serbia). They preserve liturgical and theological traditions in a diaspora context.

Comparison Axes (from manifest)

  • Ecclesiology: Episcopal, synodal; unity through bishops in communion
  • Soteriology: Theosis (participation in divine life)
  • Sacramental Theology: Seven sacraments; liturgy-centered life
  • Social Ethics: Emphasis on tradition, family, fasting, and ascetic discipline
  • Ecumenical Theology: Active in dialogue, careful about dogmatic boundaries
  • Swedish Context: Minority church, serving immigrant and convert communities

Notes

Official bodies include the Orthodox Metropolis of Sweden (Ecumenical Patriarchate) and local parishes connected to other patriarchates. The Orthodox presence in Sweden is marked by both cultural preservation and growing ecumenical engagement.
