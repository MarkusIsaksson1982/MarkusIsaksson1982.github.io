Church of Sweden (Svenska kyrkan)

Theological Foundations

Core Lutheran confessional documents with Swedish contextual adaptations

  • Church Order (Kyrkoordning): https://www.svenskakyrkan.se/kyrkoordning

Key Theological Distinctives: Lutheran emphasis on grace alone (sola gratia), scripture alone (sola scriptura), with episcopal structure retained from pre-Reformation period. Significant theological openness including ordination of women (1958) and LGBTQ+ inclusion.

Liturgical Practices

Lutheran liturgical tradition with Swedish cultural integration
  • Service Book: https://www.svenskakyrkan.se/handbok
  • Hymnal (Psalmboken): https://www.svenskakyrkan.se/psalmer
  • Liturgical Year: https://www.svenskakyrkan.se/kyrkoaret

Sacraments: Two sacraments (Baptism, Holy Communion). Episcopal confirmation common. Strong emphasis on liturgical music and cultural traditions (Lucia, Advent, etc.).

Organizational Structure

Episcopal structure with democratic governance - unique synthesis
  • Church Assembly (Kyrkomötet): https://www.svenskakyrkan.se/kyrkomote
  • Dioceses and Bishops: https://www.svenskakyrkan.se/stift
  • Archbishop of Uppsala: https://www.svenskakyrkan.se/arkebiskop

Governance Model: Democratic synodical system with episcopal leadership. Separated from state in 2000 but retains cultural establishment role. 13 dioceses, ~3.3 million members (2025).

Social Teaching

Strong emphasis on social justice, environmental stewardship, peace-building
  • Climate and Creation Care: https://www.svenskakyrkan.se/klimat
  • Diaconal Work: https://www.svenskakyrkan.se/diakoni
  • Migration and Sanctuary: https://www.svenskakyrkan.se/migration
  • Peace and Reconciliation: https://www.svenskakyrkan.se/fred

Current Priorities: Climate action, refugee support, interfaith dialogue, gender equality, LGBTQ+ inclusion. Strong tradition of international development cooperation.

Ecumenical Relations

Leading role in ecumenical movement, multiple formal agreements

Ecumenical Leadership: Archbishop Söderblom (Nobel Peace Prize 1930) founded modern ecumenical movement. Current Archbishop Martin Modéus chairs Swedish Christian Council hosting Stockholm 2025.

Post-Christendom context with retained cultural significance
